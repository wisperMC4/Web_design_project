Warm up

Burpees

Why: They are responsible for burning a lot of fat. they are good to warm up your body since it is a fullbody exercise.

How: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. Then bend at your hips and knees to lower yourself into a squatting position. Next place your hands on the floor in front of you. Kick your feet back so that you are in the plank position. Drop all the way down to the floor. Press up with your arms and jump your feet forward. Jump straight upwards to finish the move. Then repeat for reps.

REPS: 15
SETS: 2


Push Ups

Why: They are responsible for burning a lot of fat. they are good to warm up your body since it is a fullbody exercise.

How: Position hands palms-down on the floor, approximately shoulder width apart. Lower your body. Then when your chest is touching the ground, push away from the ground.

REPS: 10
SETS: 2

Legs

Goblet Squats

Why: A squat is a strength exercise in which the trainee lowers his or her hips from a standing position and then stands back up. These are great for strengthening your legs.

How: Hold a weight at your chest using both of your hands and stand with your feet about hip-width apart. Then lower your legs to be parallel

REPS: 15
SETS: 3


Step up

Why: Step ups are good for toning your glutes.

How: Put one leg on a raised object. Then raise your other leg up and repeat for reps

REPS: 10 Per leg
SETS: 3


Lunges

Why: This is another great leg exercise to help build strength in your quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings, calves, and core.

How: Start by standing up straight with your feet hip-width apart and flat on the ground. ake a big step forward with your right leg. Lower your body until your right knee is at a 90-degree angle. Push yourself upwards with your right foot, then repeat for reps on each

REPS: 20
SETS: 3

Arms

Bicep Curls

Why: When done properly bicep curls are one of the simplest weightlifting moves to employ, and valuable to anyone seeking to build or maintain arm strength and muscle tone.

How: Start small and work your way up the dumbbell scale as you progress as what many beginers do is they lift a weight that they are unable to handle properly to show off or be macho. It is best to first work on your form and then on the amount you lift. you want to avoid using momentum to lift the weight as this doesnt give you and muscle growth. Try to keep your elbows in one place.

REPS: 5 Per Arm
SETS: 3

How: Lean your body forward into the bar and lift up and down the bar this will help build your triceps up

REPS: 5
SETS: 3


Dumbell Row


Why: The one-arm dumbbell row is a great medium-intensity exercise that helps to build muscles in your mid back and triceps.

How: Place a dumbbell onto a flat exercise bench below your chest. Place your right knee on the flat exercise bench. Reach forward and grab the front of the bench with your right hand. Straighten your back and keep your head facing downward. Lift the weight directly up to the side of your torso. Clench your back muscles while holding the weight at your chest. Lower the weight back down to the floor slowly. Then switch so that your left knee is on the bench and lift with your right arm.

REPS: 10 Per Arm
SETS: 3

Shoulders

Lateral Raise

Why: Lateral raises are simple yet effective exercises that can tone your shoulder muscles and improve your physical strength. Lateral raises can be done virtually anywhere and at any time of day. Done properly and often enough, performing lateral raises builds muscle on the shoulder cap.

How: Hold a dumbbell in both hands and at your sides. Raise the dumbbells out to the sides, just below shoulder-height. Hold the raise for two to three seconds with a slight bend in your elbow. Then lower your arms slowly back to the start position. Keep your arms away from your body. Repeat the move by raising your elbows up and out to the sides.

REPS: 10
SETS: 3


Sholder Press


Why: Shoulder presses are a standard and very popular exercises. Ultimately, though, by preparing yourself properly, performing a shoulder press in a correct way, and integrating shoulder presses into your exercise routine, you'll successfully master this important exercise.

How: Stand up behind the bar. Pick up the bar. Roll your shoulder blades back and position your arms. Push the bar up over your head for about a second. Then bring the bar back down, gently.

REPS: 10
SETS: 3

Core

Russian Twist

Why: Russian twists are great for strengthening your oblique and lower back muscles in addition to your abdominal muscles.

How: Place a mat or towel on the floor. Elevate your upper body. Your upper body should be at a 45-degree angle to the floor.With your arms extended in front of you, twist your torso to the right side. Twist until your arms are parallel to the floor. Hold the position for a second or two and breathe out. Then, move back to the starting position and repeat the same technique for the left side.

REPS: 10
SETS: 3


sit ups


Why: Doing sit-ups is a quick way to get stronger abdominal muscles. However, they must be done properly to avoid any injury to your spine and the neck and head muscles.

How: Have your knees bent and the balls of your feet and heels placed flat on the ground. Place your hands on opposing shoulders, so that your arms are crossed over your chest, or behind your head. Tighten your abdominal muscles gently by drawing in your belly button to your spine.

REPS: 15
SETS: 2
